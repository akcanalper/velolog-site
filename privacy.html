VeloLog Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 3, 2026

VeloLog ("we", "the app") is a bike maintenance tracking application. This policy explains what data we collect, how we use it, and your rights.

Data we collect

When you create an account we collect your email address. As you use the app we store the bike information you enter (nickname, brand, model, year) and your service records (date, odometer reading, replaced parts, notes). Bike photos are stored only on your device and are never uploaded to our servers.

Strava data

If you connect your Strava account, VeloLog reads your bike list and total distance (odometer) from the Strava API, solely to calculate wear since your last service. Strava data is fetched live, is not permanently stored on our servers, and is never used for marketing, advertising, or shared with third parties, in accordance with the Strava API Agreement. You can disconnect Strava at any time; revoking access at strava.com/settings/apps immediately stops all access.

How we use your data

Your data is used to provide the core functionality of the app: tracking maintenance and part wear across your bikes. If you have given explicit consent, we may use your own service records (never Strava data) to show you relevant product suggestions. You can withdraw this consent at any time in Settings.

Storage and security

Account data is stored on Supabase infrastructure with row-level security: each user can only access their own records. Data is encrypted in transit (TLS).

Your rights (GDPR / KVKK)

You may request access to, correction of, or deletion of your personal data at any time. Deleting your account removes all associated data permanently. For any request, contact us at akcan.alper@gmail.com.

Children

VeloLog is not directed at children under 13 and we do not knowingly collect data from them.

Changes

We may update this policy; material changes will be announced in the app.
